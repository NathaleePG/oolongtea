Oolong Tea Therapy

September 5, 2017

A lot of people know that drinking tea might be better than drinking coffee but some people don’t really understand how beneficial this beverage really is. Hence, they skip it down the grocery isles or ignore it in their pantries and completely miss the opportunity of obtaining a healthier well-being. In this blog entry, I’m going to share with you why tea, especially oolong tea, should be an added supplement to your diet for a healthier and better you.

Oolong tea is one of the healthiest and most affordable beverages you could have in your pantries, one that your body will definitely thank you for after nurturing it with one of nature’s finest ingredients. I love this natural tea drink because it has a toasty aroma, with a beautiful nutty taste doing its justice.

What is Oolong Tea?

Oolong tea is a traditional Chinese drink and is derived from the same plant leaf as green tea and black tea, known as the Camellia sinensis. It goes through a semi-fermention process and is then dried under the sun. It partakes a medium level of enzymatic fermentation, unlike green tea and black tea which undergo low and high fermentations respectively. Overall, oolong tea has high polyphenol concentrations due to its processing or in other words, has high anti-oxidative properties.

Ohhmmmmm……

A hot brew of oolong tea served in a cozy or traditional cup is my favourite way to enjoy this therapeutic beverage. It’s flavour is much deeper and its aroma is much more intense bringing all of your senses together and at peace.

Drinking it cold with some ice, milk, honey, lemon or fruit is also a fun way to bring some personality into this tea and is definitely a thirst quencher during the hot summer months.

Benefits

1) Great for weight loss management
Those who eat a high fat diet typically have a low diversity of gut bacteria (the good kind) in their digestive system–NOT GOOD! This definitely could cause a lot of digestive problems and other diseases such as obesity. However, research shows that drinking a cup of oolong tea on a daily basis can actually reverse this by enhancing bacteria diversity and therefore increasing your metabolism and enhancing weight loss effects.



2) Great for the heart!
Research shows that oolong tea helps to deplete hypertriglyceridemia and hypercholesterolemia, therefore lowering cholesterol and preventing life threatening heart diseases.



3) Prevents diabetes
Drinking oolong tea actually controls high blood glucose levels, as research shows that it decreases hyperglycemia. If you want to cut down on the sugar, drinking some oolong tea could help.



4) Stress Relief Therapy
High stress levels brings negative effects to our physiological and mental well being. It is well known through various studies that oolong tea helps maintain your stress hormones, lowering vigil stress, shoulder stiffness and eye fatigue. This would be a great alternative to coffee as it not only gives you energy, but it also helps you to relax and lower your stress levels especially during long hours of work.



5) Prevents cancer
Studies show that oolong tea contains polyphenols that induce the cell death of two cancer cell lines (research cancer cells). Therefore, drinking this tea lowers the risk of colon cancer and other various forms of cancer.



6) Healthy bones and teeth
Quite a lot of research has shown that oolong tea maintains strong bones and teeth as it is rich in vitamins, minerals and organic compounds. They have shown that tea drinkers tend to have higher mineral bone densities than non-tea drinkers. Therefore, oolong tea also prevents osteoporosis especially in older adults.



7) Great for a morning and afternoon boost!
Oolong tea naturally contains caffeine which can be an awesome and maybe even better alternative to coffee which is known to be a diuretic. Besides, research shows that coffee doesn’t have that many good physiological effects on the body anyway. But this can be great for those early sleepy mornings or tiring afternoons when you just need a boost of energy. But don’t over dose on it because too much caffeine can be bad for your health. Take everything in healthy moderations.



8) Dermatitis and Acne
Dermatitis is a inflammatory skin disease, one type known as eczema. Studies show that drinking oolong tea is an effective way to treat dermatitis because of it’s polyphenols and catechins that are antiallergenics. The antioxidants in your body can also flush out the toxins out of your body, therefore helping to maintain clear and healthy skin.
